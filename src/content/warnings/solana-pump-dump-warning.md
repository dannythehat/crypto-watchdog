## Quick summary
A sharp rise in Pump and Dump Token Schemes on Solana DEXs has been identified. These operations involve the rapid creation of tokens designed to fail after a brief price spike. Investors are losing funds as anonymous creators exit positions within hours of launch.

This crypto scam warning highlights the risks of trading new assets on decentralised exchanges. These platforms often lack the listing requirements found on major services. Without oversight, malicious actors can easily manipulate prices and drain liquidity from unsuspecting participants.

## What happened

Recent activity on the Solana network shows a pattern of coordinated market manipulation. Fraudsters launch tokens with no utility and use social media to create artificial demand. Once the price reaches a specific target, the creators sell their entire supply.

The original alert notes that these schemes often conclude within forty eight hours. This speed prevents many investors from reacting before the value drops to zero. The surge in these incidents suggests a systematic approach by organised groups using automated tools.

Many of these tokens are promoted through Telegram groups and Twitter influencers. These individuals often receive payment or free tokens to generate hype. This creates a false sense of legitimacy for projects that have no underlying product or team.

The impact on the Solana ecosystem is significant. While the network offers low fees and high speeds, these features also benefit scammers. They can deploy hundreds of tokens for minimal cost, increasing the frequency of these attacks.

## How this scam or exploit works

The process begins with the deployment of a new smart contract on Solana. The creator typically retains a massive portion of the total supply. This concentration of power allows them to control the price movement with very little capital.

Aggressive marketing follows the launch. Influencers claim that the token will provide guaranteed returns or massive gains. They use high pressure tactics to encourage immediate buying. This creates a Fear Of Missing Out among retail investors.

As people buy the token, the price rises on decentralised exchanges. The creators may use wash trading to make the volume appear higher than it actually is. This deception makes the token look like a popular and trending investment.

When the price peaks, the creators dump their holdings into the liquidity pool. Because they hold the majority of the supply, this action exhausts the available funds. The price crashes instantly, and the creators disappear with the proceeds.

In some cases, the liquidity pool is not locked. This means the creator can remove the underlying assets at any time. This is often called a rug pull. It leaves investors holding worthless tokens that cannot be sold or traded.

## Red flags to check before connecting a wallet or depositing

The age of the token contract is a primary indicator of risk. Most pump and dump schemes involve tokens created within the last forty eight hours. If a project has no history, it should be treated with extreme caution.

Holder distribution is another critical factor. Use a block explorer to see if a single wallet holds more than half of the supply. A high concentration of tokens in one hand is a major warning sign of potential manipulation.

Check the status of the liquidity pool. If the liquidity is unlocked or controlled by a single address, the project is unsafe. Legitimate projects usually lock liquidity for a set period to prove they cannot suddenly withdraw funds.

Be wary of aggressive social media promotion. Any project promising guaranteed returns is likely a fraud. Genuine blockchain projects focus on technology and utility rather than price speculation and hype.
