## Quick summary

The CONDO token on the Base network has collapsed by 99 percent in a suspected rug pull. On 11 March 2024, the project developer sold a massive cache of tokens. This action drained nearly 1 million dollars in liquidity. Investors are now left with worthless assets.

This incident serves as a stark crypto scam warning for those trading memecoins. The speed of the collapse highlights the risks of unaudited projects. Most victims will find their funds are permanently lost. Recovery is highly unlikely in these decentralised environments.

## What happened

The CONDO token launched on 9 March 2024 on the Base network. It leveraged social media hype and ties to the Milady community to attract buyers. Within forty eight hours, the project reached a significant valuation. This growth was driven by speculative trading and aggressive online promotion.

On 11 March, blockchain security firm PeckShield detected a massive sell order. The wallet identified as the token deployer sold 212 million CONDO tokens. This transaction was swapped for approximately 247 Ether. The sudden withdrawal of value caused an immediate price crash of over 99 percent.

The deployer wallet address 0x836...192b effectively emptied the liquidity pool. This left the remaining token holders with no way to sell their assets. The total loss to the community is estimated at 997,000 dollars. It is a textbook example of a developer exit scam.

## Why this matters

The Base network is a popular layer two solution for Ethereum. While the network itself is legitimate, its low fees attract malicious actors. Scammers can launch tokens with minimal costs and target retail investors. This event shows that even trending networks host significant risks.

Rug pulls damage the reputation of the entire decentralised finance ecosystem. They discourage new participants and invite heavy regulatory scrutiny. For the individual, the loss of capital can be devastating. Understanding these mechanics is essential for anyone using [vetted crypto platform reviews](/reviews) to find safe entry points.

## How the scam or exploit works

A rug pull is a premeditated theft involving liquidity pools. The developer first creates a new token and keeps a large supply. They then pair this token with a valuable asset like Ether. This pair is deposited into a decentralised exchange to allow trading.

Once the pool has enough Ether from public buyers, the developer strikes. They dump their massive supply of tokens into the pool. This action extracts all the valuable Ether and replaces it with worthless project tokens. The liquidity is gone and the price hits zero instantly.

This specific Condo (CONDO) scam relied on social engineering. By mimicking popular internet subcultures, the creators built false trust. They used the fear of missing out to ensure the pool was deep. When the exit happened, it was too fast for any human to react.

## Red flags to check first

Investors should have noticed the lack of a smart contract audit. Reputable projects pay security firms to check their code for backdoors. CONDO had no such verification. This left the door open for the developer to manipulate the supply or the liquidity pool at will.

Concentrated ownership was another massive warning sign. On chain data showed the deployer held a dominant percentage of the supply. When one person controls the majority of a token, they control the price. This is a primary indicator of a potential rug pull.

The project lacked any real world utility or a technical whitepaper. It existed solely as a vehicle for speculation. Projects without a clear purpose often rely on hype to sustain value. You can learn more about identifying these traps in our [crypto safety education](/blog) section.
