## The exchange most Crypto.com users never use

Here's the quiet truth about Crypto.com: the **App** everyone downloads is the *expensive* way to buy crypto, and the **Exchange** — the same login, one tab away — is the cheap way. The App's one-tap "instant buy" bakes a spread of roughly **0.5–1%+** into the price. The Exchange uses real **order books** with maker/taker fees around **0.00%–0.075%**.

If you only ever buy £20 of Bitcoin once, the difference is noise. If you trade with any regularity, switching from the App to the Exchange is the single most valuable thing you can do on the platform.

## Fees, liquidity and CRO

Exchange fees scale with your 30-day volume and how much **CRO** you stake. At higher tiers, maker fees approach zero. That's genuinely competitive — in the same league as Kraken and the [Coinbase](/reviews/coinbase) Advanced flow.

The trade-off is the CRO lock-up: to unlock the best tiers you tie up the platform's own token, which carries price risk. Only do it if you trade enough to earn the discount back, and never lock more than you'd be comfortable holding regardless.

Liquidity is strong on major pairs (BTC, ETH, the big alts and stablecoin markets). You won't find the long tail of obscure tokens that Binance lists, but for mainstream trading it's more than deep enough.

## Safety and withdrawals

The Exchange sits inside the same security and compliance framework as the rest of Crypto.com: a **MiCA licence**, **proof-of-reserves attestations**, mostly **cold storage**, 2FA, an anti-phishing code and a withdrawal whitelist. In our checks, crypto withdrawals cleared within **minutes to a couple of hours** once the address was whitelisted, with the standard **24-hour hold** on any new address.

It is, like every centralised exchange, **custodial** — Crypto.com holds the keys. Trade here, but move long-term holdings to the self-custody [DeFi Wallet](/reviews/crypto-com) or a [hardware wallet](/crypto-wallets).

## Verdict

A genuinely good, low-fee exchange hiding behind a famous app. If you're going to use Crypto.com, use the Exchange — **80/100, green** — and read the [full Crypto.com review](/reviews/crypto-com) for how the rest of the ecosystem stacks up.
