## Real yield, for once

Most "yield" in crypto is just new money paying old money, or a token printing rewards until it collapses. **Ondo Finance** is one of the few exceptions: it tokenizes **real US Treasuries**, so the yield behind products like **OUSG** and **USDY** comes from actual short-term government debt. That's what "real-world asset" (RWA) tokenization is supposed to mean.

It's built with **institutional partners and custodians**, the contracts are **audited**, and the disclosures are public — a world away from the [Ponzi yield](/blog/defi-lending-real-yield-vs-ponzi-yield-2026) we warn about.

## The catch

Access is **gated by KYC and jurisdiction**, so not everyone can mint. You also carry **smart-contract risk** and **issuer/custody dependence**, and because the yield tracks interest rates, it **falls when rates fall**. Tokenized RWAs are also a young, evolving regulatory area.

## Verdict

For conservative, transparent, real-world yield on-chain, Ondo is the sector leader — **85/100, green**. Read the specific product docs, confirm eligibility for your country, and hold in self-custody. See the wider [tokenized assets](/tokenized-assets) category for alternatives.

> Not financial advice. Confirm eligibility and tax treatment before buying.
