## The training-wheels NFT marketplace

Most NFT marketplaces assume you already own a self-custody wallet, understand gas, and can spot a malicious approval. Crypto.com's takes the opposite approach: **buy with a card, pay no gas, skip the wallet setup entirely.** For a complete beginner, that removes nearly every way to get burned on day one.

The price of that comfort is **control**. It's a **custodial, curated** marketplace, so the catalogue is smaller and less liquid than [OpenSea](/reviews/opensea) or Blur, and you don't have the full self-custody ownership that defines the open NFT world.

## Convenience vs control

The convenience is real: no gas fees, card payments, and everything inside the account you already have. The curation also quietly reduces your exposure to the most obvious scam mints that plague open marketplaces.

But if you're a serious collector, the limits bite. Liquidity is thinner, the selection is narrower, and "your" NFT lives inside Crypto.com's custody unless the collection supports withdrawal to an external wallet — which varies. If self-custody matters to you, **check that a collection is withdrawable before you buy.**

## Safety

The marketplace inherits the broader group's protections — a licensed operator, proof-of-reserves attestations, account 2FA. That makes the *platform* trustworthy. What it can't do is make **NFTs** a safe investment: they're volatile, frequently illiquid, and easy to overpay for. Treat any spend here as high-risk discretionary money. Our [NFT safety guide](/blog/nft-safety-guide-2026) covers the wider traps.

## Verdict

A genuinely friendly on-ramp to NFTs that trades openness for safety rails — **70/100, caution**. Great for a first toe in the water; limiting if you want to collect seriously. For the rest of the ecosystem, read the [full Crypto.com review](/reviews/crypto-com).
