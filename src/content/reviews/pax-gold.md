## Is PAX Gold (PAXG) safe?

PAX Gold is, in our view, the **benchmark for tokenized gold**. Each PAXG token represents **one fine troy ounce** of London Good Delivery gold held in professional vaults, and the issuer — **Paxos** — is a **New York State (NYDFS) regulated trust company** that publishes regular third-party **attestations** of the gold backing. You can even look up the specific bars behind the supply.

That combination of **regulation + verifiable reserves + self-custody** is rare. Most ways to "own gold" ask you to trust a balance on someone's screen; PAXG lets you check the backing and hold the token in your own wallet.

## The risks worth knowing

It isn't risk-free. You're still trusting **Paxos and its vault partners** to hold the metal (issuer/custody risk), **physical redemption** is only available at large full-bar thresholds, and — obviously — the **gold price itself can fall**. Tax treatment of tokenized gold also varies by country, so check your local rules.

## Verdict

For on-chain gold exposure without storing bullion yourself, PAXG is our top pick — **88/100, green**. Verify the latest attestation on the Paxos transparency page, hold it in self-custody, and treat gold as the volatile asset it is.
