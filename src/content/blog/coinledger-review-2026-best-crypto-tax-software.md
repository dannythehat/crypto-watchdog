## A deeper look at how tax authorities are tracking crypto in 2026

To understand why CoinLedger has become essential rather than optional, you have to understand what changed on the enforcement side.

In 2025, the US Treasury finalised the broker reporting rules under section 6045. Beginning with the 2025 tax year, every centralised exchange operating in the US must issue Form 1099 DA to both the customer and the IRS. That form lists every taxable disposal during the year. The IRS already has the data before you file.

In the UK, HMRC has run nudge letter campaigns to identified Coinbase, Binance and Kraken users every year since 2021. The letters specifically reference data shared by the exchange under the international information exchange treaty. By 2027, the OECD Crypto Asset Reporting Framework expands this globally across more than 50 participating countries.

What this means in plain English: tax authorities know what you traded, when, and roughly how much. If your filed return does not match the data they already hold, you become an audit target. The cost of getting it wrong now far exceeds the cost of getting it right.

That is the real value of CoinLedger. Not just convenience. Compliance insurance.
