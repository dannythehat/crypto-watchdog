## Copy trading cashback and lead trader rewards

Copy trading is Bitget's flagship product and gets the biggest standing promotion budget. There are two sides to it.

**For copiers (followers):** Bitget periodically runs **profit-share rebates** that refund part of the performance fee — commonly around 10% of a copier's profit — back to the follower during campaign periods. The effective fee during a promotion can therefore be lower than the headline rate.

**For lead traders:** Programmes reward lead traders based on copier volume and performance, with the strongest performers earning ongoing bonuses on top of profit-share fees.

Here is the part the marketing skips: **cashback reduces your fee, never your risk.** A highly leveraged copy trade can be liquidated quickly regardless of any rebate. If you copy trade:

- Treat rebates as a small margin of safety, not as a reason to take more leverage.

## Launchpad and Launchpool farms

Bitget runs both a **Launchpad** (commit BGB or USDT toward a token-sale allocation) and a **Launchpool** (stake assets to farm a new token).

- **Launchpool** is generally the lower-risk of the two because you keep your principal — only the farmed token carries price risk. Advertised APYs can briefly look very high but typically decay quickly as more participants join.
- **Launchpad** allocations expose you directly to a brand-new token's price, which can be highly volatile.

Before farming or buying any new token, do basic due diligence on supply concentration, liquidity and contract behaviour. New listings are also a favourite theme for scammers running copycat "opportunities" — the kind of pattern we document in our [CryptoMine Pro scam warning](/warnings/cryptomine-pro-scam-warning). If an "official Bitget farm" reaches you by unsolicited DM, it is almost certainly fake.
