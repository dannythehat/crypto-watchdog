Cloud mining is the single most-abused product category in crypto. Of every cloud-mining offer we have audited at Crypto Watchdog, the overwhelming majority — somewhere around 95% — are not mining anything at all.

They are deposit-collection schemes structured to look like mining, with returns paid from new deposits until the inevitable collapse. The [FTC''s consumer protection bulletins](https://consumer.ftc.gov/articles/what-know-about-cryptocurrency-and-scams) have flagged the pattern repeatedly. The [SEC''s 2024 enforcement actions](https://www.sec.gov/securities-topics/crypto-assets) include several cloud-mining cases that ran for years before collapsing.

This article exists because the marketing is so polished and the math is so opaque that even cautious users get caught. We''ve documented one such operation in our [CryptoMine Pro warning](/reviews/cryptomine-pro). The structure is depressingly consistent. So is the way you spot it.

## Why the economics almost never work

Bitcoin mining in 2026 is a brutal commodity business. The [global hash rate](https://www.blockchain.com/explorer/charts/hash-rate) sits above 700 EH/s, electricity costs determine profitability to the cent, and even tier-1 industrial operators (Marathon, Riot, CleanSpark) operate on margins that are visible quarter-by-quarter in their public filings.

A retail customer paying for a "cloud mining contract" is, in the best case, a layer of fees on top of an already thin margin.

In the realistic case, no actual mining is happening at all. The "daily yield" is just a withdrawal-paced drip from incoming deposits. The platform stays solvent as long as new money exceeds withdrawals. When it doesn''t, withdrawals get "temporarily disabled" for "system maintenance" — and the operator vanishes.

This is the same Ponzi pattern we documented in [the myth of guaranteed returns](/blog/the-myth-of-guaranteed-returns-in-crypto-what-you-need-to-know-in-2026-2026-04-15).

## The seven patterns that mark a scam

| # | Pattern | What it looks like | Why it''s a Ponzi tell |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Fixed daily / weekly returns | "Earn 1.5% daily" | Real mining returns vary daily with hash rate, fees, and price |
| 2 | No verifiable hardware or facility | Stock-photo "data centres" | Real operators publish facility addresses and walk-throughs |
| 3 | "Upgrade your contract" demands | Pay extra to access withdrawals | Withdrawals from real operations don''t require new fees |
| 4 | Referral commissions of 5%+ | Pyramid-like multi-level structure | Recruitment is the actual revenue engine |
| 5 | Anonymous team / no real company | No registry filing, no named CEO | Real industrial mining is regulated and named |
| 6 | "Free" daily mining demo | Tiny payouts to build trust before bigger asks | Classic deposit-funnel mechanic |
| 7 | Withdrawals require "tax" or "verification fee" | Final extraction before disappearance | The exit-scam''s last move |

Any one of these on its own is a strong negative signal. Two or more together is decisive. The whole pattern is summarised in our [10 red-flags guide](/blog/how-to-spot-crypto-scam-2026).

## What real mining-adjacent businesses look like

There is a narrow legitimate slice. It is not retail-friendly and that is precisely the point.

| Category | What it is | Realistic return | How to verify |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Public mining stocks** | Nasdaq / NYSE-listed industrial miners | Equity returns tracking BTC | SEC filings, audited accounts |
| **Hosted mining** | You own the rig, they host it | BTC return minus electricity & hosting | Walk the facility, see your machine |
| **Mining pool participation** | Run your own ASIC, share rewards | Variable, real | Your wallet, your power bill |
| **Cloud "mining" platforms** | Almost always not mining | "Fixed APY" — fictional | Cannot be verified |

If you want exposure to mining economics, the boring answer is owning shares of a public miner. The slightly less boring answer is buying a real ASIC and either running it yourself or hosting it at a named, walkable facility.

Anything advertised on Telegram with a referral programme is the third row.

## The seven questions that filter every cloud-mining offer

1. **Is the company registered, named, and findable in a public registry?** Anonymous operators are not running data centres.
2. **Can you visit the facility, or see independent walk-throughs?** Real industrial sites are documented.
3. **Are returns variable and tied to the BTC price + hash rate?** Fixed returns are an immediate fail.
4. **Is the contract priced in line with realistic ASIC economics?** A back-of-envelope check using public hash-price data ($/TH/day from [Hashrate Index](https://hashrateindex.com/)) will reveal whether the maths even possibly works.
5. **Is there a multi-level referral structure?** Real businesses don''t need 10% MLM commissions.
6. **Are withdrawals processed without "fees" or "upgrades"?** Demand for additional payment to access funds is the exit-scam tell.
7. **Has the platform been audited by anyone other than itself?** "Audited" by the operator''s own accountant is not an audit.

If a platform fails on any of 1, 3, 6, walk away. If it fails on more than one of the rest, walk away. The 15-minute version of this check is also bundled into our [submission portal](/submit) — send us a platform and we''ll publish a full audit.

## What you''re actually betting on if you ignore the warnings

Cloud-mining marketing leans heavily on FOMO ("Bitcoin halving 2028 — get in before the next bull cycle"). The implicit pitch is: even if it''s a bit dodgy, the upside is so big it doesn''t matter. That logic doesn''t work.

In a Ponzi the upside is fictional — you are not actually long Bitcoin, you are long the operator''s willingness to keep paying. When that ends, the asset price is irrelevant.

If you want long Bitcoin exposure, buy Bitcoin and hold it on a [hardware wallet](/blog/hardware-wallets-2026-buyers-guide). If you want operating leverage on Bitcoin, buy a basket of public miners. If you want to mine, buy a real ASIC.

There is no fourth thing that gives you those returns with none of the inconvenience. That''s the entire trick.
