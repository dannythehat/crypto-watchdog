## How The Ecosystem Works (In Plain English)

What makes Aurum's model unusual is the combination it bundles together: **revenue-generating products, user self-custody, and institutional capital** in one stack. Crypto projects usually pick one.

Here's the flow for a retail user. You sign up via the Aurum app, complete KYC, and choose a package that powers the Ex-AI Bot. ## ✅ The Hacken Audit: What It Proves And What It Doesn't

[Hacken](https://hacken.io/) is one of the more respected smart-contract and cybersecurity audit firms in crypto. Its client list has included major exchanges, Layer 1s, custodians and infrastructure providers — the sort of clients that cannot afford a single critical vulnerability slipping through.

Passing a Hacken engagement means a serious third party has spent weeks reviewing the codebase, threat-modelling the architecture, probing access controls and signing off on the result in writing. That is not a process you volunteer for if your plan is to vanish. Scam operators tend to do the opposite: anonymous teams, no audits, no compliance, no public stack. They avoid external scrutiny because they cannot survive it.

Here's the honest caveat. An audit is a snapshot, not a permanent guarantee. It covers a specific codebase at a specific point in time; later changes can introduce new issues, and even a clean report doesn't speak to off-chain operational risk or how the business is run. There's also a verification step still outstanding: at the time of writing, we have seen the audit referenced in Aurum's updates but the full public report had not yet been published. Until that report is live and we can read the scope and findings ourselves, treat "passed a Hacken audit" as **company-stated and pending independent confirmation** rather than fully verified.

With that flagged, a Hacken sign-off is still one of the clearer *behavioural* signals we weigh under our [audit methodology](/methodology). It moves the needle. It does not remove risk.
