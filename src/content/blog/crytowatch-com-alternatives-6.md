## How to Choose Between Them

The six tools here split into two camps. The first is prevention: checking a platform or a swap before you commit money. That's Crypto Watchdog, ScamSignals and AntiSwap, and for most individuals it's where the value is. The second is investigation and institutional risk: Chainalysis, CoinStructive and Bitpulse, which are powerful but priced and built for organisations.

If you only have time for one habit, make it the pre-deposit check. The data backs this up. Scam Sniffer reported that crypto phishing losses fell 83% to $83.85m in 2025, with the victim count dropping to 106,106 from around 332,000 the year before ([Scam Sniffer 2025 report](https://drops.scamsniffer.io/scam-sniffer-2025-crypto-phishing-losses-fall-83-to-84-million/)). That fall is encouraging, but the same report notes the drainer ecosystem is still active, with new operators stepping in as old ones exit. The threat hasn't gone away. It's just smaller and quieter, which is arguably more dangerous if you've let your guard down.

A few honest things to weigh before you settle on a tool:

- **Pricing transparency.** Several of these don't publish prices. That's normal for enterprise software, but it does mean you can't compare on cost without a sales call. For an individual, the free options remove that friction entirely.
- **Who it's actually for.** A forensic investigations firm is overkill for checking whether an exchange pays out. A consumer scam-checker won't trace stolen funds across six chains for a court case. Picking the wrong category wastes time and money.
- **Where the data comes from.** Tools that lean on user reports are only as current as their last report. Tools that run live tests or reconcile chain data are less dependent on the crowd, but cover fewer platforms. Neither is wrong; just know which you're getting.
- **What it won't do.** None of these will recover lost funds on their own, and none can promise a platform is safe forever. A green signal today is a snapshot, not a guarantee. Re-check before any large deposit.
