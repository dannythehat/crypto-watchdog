## The "AI quant trading" red flag

MoneyFlare's public positioning is as an automated AI trading platform for users with little or no experience. That framing deserves scrutiny.

Regulators have repeatedly warned about it. The US Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) has cautioned that claims of high or **guaranteed returns from AI trading bots or trade-signal algorithms are red flags** — see the [CFTC customer advisory on AI scams](https://www.cftc.gov/LearnAndProtect/AdvisoriesAndArticles/AI_Scams.html).

The mechanics matter. A "black box" bot where you deposit capital into a hidden algorithm has a structural problem: you cannot see what, if anything, is actually being traded. In the worst cases, the dashboard shows fabricated profits to encourage bigger deposits while funds are moved elsewhere. We are **not** asserting that this is what MoneyFlare does — we cannot prove that — but the model itself is the one fraudsters most often imitate.

If you want to understand why "let the AI trade for you" pitches are so dangerous, the safest mental model is custody: who actually controls the coins? ## How to protect yourself right now

If you already have money in MoneyFlare:

1. **Stop adding funds immediately.** Do not "top up to unlock a withdrawal" — that is a common trap.
2. **Attempt a withdrawal of your full balance now**, while documenting every step with screenshots and timestamps.
