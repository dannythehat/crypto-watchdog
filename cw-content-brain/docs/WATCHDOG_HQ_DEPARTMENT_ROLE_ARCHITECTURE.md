## Core Architecture Rule

Every future role must sit inside:

- one executive owner
- one owning department
- one department manager
- one role family
- one capability boundary
- one workflow route
- one QA route
- one audit route
- one rollback and incident route

No role may self-create. No role may operate outside the Build 63 Agent Onboarding and Expansion Gate.

## Four-Layer Company Structure

Layer 1: Human Owner

Danny is the final authority for strategic direction, high-risk brand decisions, trust-rating changes, Green / Orange / Red status decisions, scam or legal wording, publication approval, affiliate approval, live-site edits, Supabase writes, Safe Apply activation, and override of failed governance gates.

Layer 2: Executive AI Layer

Executive AI roles coordinate departments and prevent drift.

Core executive roles:

- The Gaffer: Chief Orchestrator
- Gatekeeper Grace: QA and Governance Controller
- Audit Alfie: Audit Controller
- Routey Rachel: Routing Controller
- Approval Ava: Danny Review Queue Controller

Layer 3: Department Manager Layer

Department managers own queues, assignment, rejection loops, and routine report-only movement.

Managers may assign work, reject work, request evidence, route to QA, route sideways to another manager, escalate to The Gaffer, and prepare Danny-ready decision items.

Managers must not publish, edit live pages, write to Supabase, insert affiliate links, change trust ratings, approve Danny-only decisions, bypass Gatekeeper Grace, or bypass Build 63 onboarding.

Layer 4: Specialist Worker Layer

Specialists do narrow, bounded work. They may create report-only findings, outlines, page plans, content briefs, evidence notes, SEO notes, media briefs, validation reports, QA notes, and Danny review recommendations.

Specialists must not invent evidence, invent screenshots, invent testing, invent approval, publish, edit live pages, write to Supabase, insert affiliate links, change trust ratings, bypass managers, bypass QA, or bypass Danny approval.

## Department Ownership Rules

Every future task must have one primary owning department.

Support departments may assist, but ownership remains singular unless The Gaffer approves a reroute.

Examples:

- review rebuild: Content Operations owns; Research, SEO, QA, and Media support
- themed hub build: Editorial Strategy owns; Content Production, SEO, Media, QA, and Affiliate support
- crypto exchange page plan: Editorial Strategy owns; Research, SEO, Affiliate, Media, and QA support
- unsupported claim check: QA and Governance owns; Research supports
- internal link plan: SEO owns; Content Operations supports
- image or video placement plan: Media owns; Editorial and QA support
- affiliate opportunity note: Affiliate and Commercial owns; QA and The Gaffer support
- performance refresh trigger: Analytics and Lifecycle owns; SEO and Content Operations support
- serious warning page: QA and Governance owns; Research and Editorial support
- Danny decision item: Executive Orchestration owns; relevant manager and QA support

## Reporting Lines

Default reporting line:

Specialist Worker -> Department Manager -> Gatekeeper Grace where risk exists -> The Gaffer where priority or conflict exists -> Danny where CEO-level approval is required.

Page-builder reporting line:

Page Builder -> Editorial Strategy Manager or Content Operations Manager -> Template Validator -> SEO where needed -> Media where needed -> QA and Governance -> The Gaffer -> Danny only for critical decisions.

Affiliate reporting line:

Affiliate Worker -> Offer Owl -> Disclosure Daisy or Gatekeeper Grace -> The Gaffer -> Danny for material commercial decisions.

Evidence reporting line:

Evidence Checker -> Inspector Proof -> Claim Checker Colin where needed -> Gatekeeper Grace -> Danny for legal, scam, fraud, or rating-sensitive decisions.

SEO reporting line:

SEO Worker -> Rankhound -> Content Operations or Editorial Strategy -> Gatekeeper Grace if wording risk exists -> The Gaffer if priority decision exists.

Media reporting line:

Media Planner -> Media Manager or Image Iris -> Content Operations or Editorial Strategy -> Gatekeeper Grace if evidence-sensitive visuals exist.

Analytics reporting line:

Analytics Worker -> Metric Molly -> SEO or Content Operations -> The Gaffer where prioritisation is needed.

## Current-Now Versus Future-Later Roles

Current-now allowed role behaviour:

- plan
- draft locally
- validate locally
- route internally
- create report-only outputs
- prepare Danny review queues
- update docs through approved PRs
- recommend next steps

Future-later blocked role behaviour:

- publish
- edit live pages
- write to Supabase
- insert affiliate links
- change ratings
- call external APIs
- generate or download media
- send outreach
- send email
- apply changes to live systems
- operate Safe Apply
