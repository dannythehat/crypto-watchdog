## 3. Global page principles

Every CryptoWatchdog page must be:

1. Useful to a real person trying to make a safer crypto decision.
2. Clear enough for beginners without becoming patronising.
3. Evidence-led, not hype-led.
4. Structured for SEO, but never written like an SEO farm.
5. Honest about unknowns, proof gaps, and limitations.
6. Human in tone where appropriate.
7. Serious and careful on scam-loss, warning, victim, legal, safety, or high-risk pages.
8. Connected to related reviews, guides, warnings, and category hubs.
9. Built with Danny approval checkpoints before any live action.

---

## 4. Tone rules

### 4.1 Default brand voice

CryptoWatchdog should sound:

- Human
- Plain-English
- Witty where appropriate
- Useful
- Slightly sharp when calling out vague marketing or weak evidence
- Calm and trustworthy
- Not robotic
- Not corporate
- Not reckless

### 4.2 Serious-content exception

No humour, jokes, banter, sarcasm, or flippant language on:

- Scam-loss pages
- Victim-support pages
- Legal/regulatory content
- Wallet-drain warnings
- Recovery guidance
- Safety warnings
- High-risk red-flag articles
- Any page where a reader may have lost money

Those pages must be calm, careful, factual, and supportive.

## 6. Review page template

Use for individual platform, exchange, wallet, app, bot, casino, tool, or service reviews.

### Required structure

1. H1: Is [Brand] safe? CryptoWatchdog review
2. Short answer / quick verdict
3. Trust status panel
4. Who [Brand] is for
5. What [Brand] does
6. How [Brand] works
7. Key features
8. Fees, costs, limits, or pricing where relevant
9. Account setup / onboarding notes
10. Deposit and withdrawal notes where relevant
11. Security and custody notes
12. Regulation, licensing, or company transparency notes where relevant
13. User complaints / common concerns, evidence-led only
14. Red flags and proof gaps
15. Better alternatives / related options
16. FAQs
17. Affiliate disclosure where relevant
18. CryptoWatchdog final word

### Trust status panel fields

- Brand name
- Category
- Current status: Green / Orange / Red / Unrated / Needs more evidence
- Last reviewed date
- Evidence level
- Testing status
- Affiliate relationship: Yes / No / Not currently
- Danny approval status

### Evidence rules

Do not claim something was tested unless there is actual test evidence.

Use phrases such as:

- We have not yet completed a live deposit/withdrawal test.
- We found limited public evidence for this claim.
- This section needs Danny review before publication.
- This is a proof gap, not automatically a red flag.

### Review page must not

- Accuse a brand of being a scam without strong evidence and Danny approval.
- Invent testing.
- Invent screenshots.
- Invent regulation.
- Insert affiliate links without approval.
- Change trust rating automatically.

## 7. Category / themed hub page template

Use for category pages such as wallets, exchanges, crypto casinos, AI trading bots, DeFi wallets, staking platforms, or crypto tax tools.

### Purpose

A themed hub page should act as a useful authority page, not a thin category index.

### Required structure

1. H1: Best / safest / explained [category] pages, depending on search intent
2. Plain-English category explanation
3. Who this category is for
4. How this type of product works
5. Why people use it
6. Main risks and red flags
7. How CryptoWatchdog reviews this category
8. Trusted / reviewed brand boxes
9. Comparison table
10. Related reviews
11. Related guides
12. Related warnings
13. Related blog posts
14. FAQs
15. CryptoWatchdog final word

### Hub page principle

A themed hub page should help the reader understand the category before pushing them into individual reviews.

It should answer:

- What is this thing?
- Why would someone use it?
- What can go wrong?
- How do I compare options?
- Which reviews should I read next?
- What should I avoid?

## 19. SEO heading / keyword / meta rules

### H1 rules

Use one clear H1 per page.

The H1 should match search intent without becoming spammy.

### Heading rules

Headings should help readers scan the page.

Avoid repeating the same keyword in every heading.

### Meta title rules

Meta titles should be clear, clickable, and not misleading.

### Meta description rules

Meta descriptions should explain the page value and include the key topic naturally.

### Keyword rules

Keywords must fit the page naturally.

No keyword stuffing.

Safety and evidence language must be accurate.

---

## 20. Internal and external linking rules

### Internal links

Use internal links to connect:

- Category hubs to reviews
- Reviews to related guides
- Warnings to safety guides
- Blog posts to evergreen pages
- Comparisons to individual reviews

### External links

External links should support claims, definitions, regulation, public evidence, or source material.

External links should not be used as decoration.

### Link rules

- Do not invent source links.
- Do not over-link.
- Do not link to suspicious sites without clear reason and caution.
- Serious claims need evidence.
