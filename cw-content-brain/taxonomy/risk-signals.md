# Risk Signal Taxonomy

## Rating Language

- `green`: no material risk signals found in the reviewed evidence, with normal caution still advised.
- `orange`: notable concerns, weak transparency, unresolved complaints, or incomplete evidence.
- `red`: strong evidence of serious user risk, regulatory action, impersonation, fund loss patterns, or deceptive conduct.

## Severity Language

- `medium`: monitor closely; risk is plausible but limited or not yet well corroborated.
- `high`: avoid new deposits or commitments until evidence improves.
- `critical`: urgent warning; users should avoid or exit exposure where possible.

## Core Risk Signals

### Identity and Transparency

- unclear company name or jurisdiction
- unverifiable leadership or ownership
- mismatched domain, brand, or registration records
- anonymous operators handling user funds

### Financial and Custody Risk

- withdrawal blocks or unusual withdrawal fees
- guaranteed returns or unrealistic yield claims
- pressure to deposit more funds before withdrawal
- commingled custody or unclear wallet controls

### Compliance and Legal Risk

- regulator warning or enforcement notice
- false license claims
- restricted-market targeting
- missing terms, privacy policy, or risk disclosures

### Technical and Security Risk

- cloned interface or impersonation
- insecure authentication or account recovery process
- unaudited contracts presented as safe
- suspicious token approvals or wallet-draining flows

### Reputation and Complaint Pattern

- repeated unresolved complaints
- copied reviews or manipulated testimonials
- sudden domain changes after complaints
- affiliate-heavy promotion without risk disclosure

## Evidence Confidence

- `low`: single weak source, anecdotal report, or incomplete documentation.
- `medium`: multiple independent user reports or partial documentary evidence.
- `high`: primary documents, regulator notices, on-chain records, or well-corroborated research.
